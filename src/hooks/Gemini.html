Medical Image Annotation Research Study

Centre for Diagnostic Futures

Welcome and thank you for your interest.

You are invited to participate in a research study investigating how people analyze medical images. Our goal is to understand how new technologies, such as Artificial Intelligence (AI), can best assist human annotators in diagnostic tasks.

Your contribution will help improve the design of future medical systems and enhance diagnostic accuracy.

What to Expect

The study consists of several parts and will take approximately 75-90 minutes to complete.

  1. Informed Consent You will first review an information sheet and provide your consent to participate.
  2. Cognitive Assessments You will complete two brief, standard assessments that measure cognitive and personality traits. This data is crucial for our research and remains strictly anonymous.
  3. Training & Practice You will receive a short (approx. 20-30 minute) training lesson on how to identify features in radiology images (e.g., signs of arthritis) and practice on 10 sample cases.
  4. Main Annotation Task You will analyze several sets of medical images. Your task is to identify if a condition is present and, in some cases, mark the region of interest.

All data collected is strictly confidential and anonymized. Your responses will be used for research purposes only.
