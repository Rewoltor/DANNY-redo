About the Study

This prestigious research project, conducted under the guidance of experts in statistics, psychometrics, Bayesian methods, machine learning, and clinical radiology, investigates how AI assistance impacts non-expert annotators' performance in detecting arthritis from joint images. As a participant, you'll help advance our understanding of human-AI collaboration in healthcare, with potential implications for improving diagnostic tools worldwide.

What to Expect

The experiment is designed with your experience in mind, featuring an intuitive interface and clear instructions to ensure a smooth, engaging process. You'll start with a short educational lecture (20-30 minutes) on radiology basics and the annotation task. This is followed by 10 practice cases to build familiarity, then a baseline phase of approximately 30 images where you'll classify arthritis (yes/no) and draw bounding boxes as needed.

After the baseline, you'll be randomly assigned to either a control group (no AI) or a treatment group (with AI assistance, including predictions, heatmaps, and overlap metrics). The test phase involves another set of about 30 images. The entire session should take 1-2 hours, and we'll measure factors like accuracy, decision time, and confidence.

Why Participate?

Your involvement supports rigorous, publishable science aimed at peer-reviewed journals. This study emphasizes ethical design, pre-specified hypotheses, and advanced statistical analyses (including Bayesian hierarchical models) for reliable insights. Participants like you are key to making healthcare AI more effective and equitable.

This study is part of a Master's thesis in sociology, focusing on human-AI interaction in medical domains. All data is confidential and used solely for research purposes.
